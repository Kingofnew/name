Ministry of Education Highlights Key Points for 2025 College Enrollment

Implementing expansion of quality undergraduate programs; severely cracking down on "Gaokao migrants."

Regarding promoting fairness in urban and rural enrollment opportunities, the "Notice" requires conscientious implementation of quality undergraduate program expansion. Driven by scientific and technological development and national strategic needs, and comprehensively considering the quality of higher education institutions and graduate employment, further optimize the layout of disciplines and majors, and the arrangement of enrollment plans.

Furthermore, the "Notice" requires improving the selection and supervision mechanism for top innovative talents. It emphasizes coordinating the implementation of various pilot projects for selecting and cultivating top innovative talents, such as the "Strong Base Plan," "Youth Class," "Elite Class," and "Recommended Student" programs, and establishing a sound mechanism that integrates selection, cultivation, and evaluation.

In actively creating a favorable exam and enrollment environment, the "Notice" reiterates that no form of publicity or hype about "Gaokao top scorers," "enrollment rates," "admission rates," or "high-scoring candidates" is allowed. It also prohibits rewarding teachers and students based on Gaokao scores and linking enrollment rates to teacher evaluations, commendations, or professional title promotions.

The "Notice" also requires that education administrative departments, enrollment examination institutions and their staff, as well as universities, high school-level schools, and their teachers, shall not organize or participate in any form of examination or enrollment training activities or paid volunteer application services. They are also prohibited from allowing educational consulting agencies, off-campus training institutions, or individuals to enter schools to conduct examination or enrollment training, consulting, and advertising activities; nor shall they promote related activities of such agencies or individuals to examinees and parents.

Scientific Diet, Boosting Gaokao | Gaokao Service Station

  • As Gaokao approaches, children's brains are like high-speed processors during preparation, requiring high-quality protein, sustained-release energy, and essential nutrients.
  • Many parents mistakenly believe that "eating a lot of meat is good for nourishment," unaware that improper diet can increase the child's gastrointestinal burden and affect their preparation state. A scientific three-meal plan should meet the demands of high-intensity brain use while avoiding overeating and drowsiness. Today, let's treat the dining table as a second desk, making every bite of food a "bonus item" and letting scientific nutrition "recharge" students' brains.
  • During the preparation and examination period, students' brains are under high tension, and their demand for energy and nutrients is higher than usual. A reasonable diet should meet the following needs: sufficient energy supply, high-quality protein, healthy fats, vitamins and minerals, and adequate hydration.

During the Gaokao period, the weather is hot, food can spoil easily, and some raw or cold foods may contain bacteria, which can easily cause food poisoning. Candidates should avoid consuming raw, cold, greasy, or spicy foods to prevent gastrointestinal discomfort.

"You Ai Gaokao Service Station"! Postal Savings Bank of China Guiyang Branch Helps Gaokao Students Pursue Their Dreams

With the 2025 Gaokao approaching, Postal Savings Bank of China Guiyang Branch actively fulfills its social responsibility by setting up "Gaokao Service Stations" at multiple "You Ai Service Stations" within its jurisdiction, providing comprehensive warm services for students and parents. Until the end of the Gaokao on June 9, 2025, dozens of You Ai Service Stations across the city will transform into "You Ai Gaokao Service Stations" to escort students on their dream journey.

During the Gaokao period, the You Ai Service Stations under the Guiyang Branch of Postal Savings Bank of China are open for free, providing facilities such as air conditioning, refrigerators, water dispensers, and microwave ovens, offering a comfortable space for students and parents to escape the heat and replenish water. To address potential emergencies during the exam, the stations are also equipped with stationery sets including gel pens, 2B pencils, erasers, and an emergency medical kit containing common medicines, band-aids, and disinfectants, ensuring students face the challenges in their best state.

29 New Undergraduate Majors Added to the Catalog

"The Ministry of Education has updated and released the 'Catalog of Undergraduate Majors in General Higher Education Institutions (2025)', adding 29 new majors. The new catalog includes 93 major categories and 845 types of majors, further strengthening the rapid response of major settings to national strategic needs and high-quality development."

— It is understood that the 29 new majors added fully reflect national strategies, market demands, and technological development drivers.

In terms of serving national strategies, new majors such as Regional and National Studies, Carbon Neutrality Science and Engineering, Marine Science and Technology, and Health and Medical Security have been added. To adapt to the forefront of scientific and technological development, new majors like Intelligent Molecular Engineering, Medical Devices and Equipment Engineering, and Spatiotemporal Information Engineering have been added. To actively meet market demands, new majors such as International Cruise Management and Aviation Sports have been added. Focusing on artificial intelligence empowering economic and social development, new majors including Artificial Intelligence Education, Intelligent Audio-Visual Engineering, and Digital Drama, among others, have been introduced.
